The project

Purpose of the project

The project aims to imitate a draft of a magazine which offers poems written by women, organized according to the historical-cultural period of their creation.
The focus is kept on lyric poetry, which has its origins in ancient Greece, where it was composed to be accompanied by melody, expressing humans' deep feelings, such as love.

The Greek muses Clio and Erato have been chosen as icons of the magazine, because they express the dimensions encompassed by this website: respectively, history and lyric poetry. Moreover, an additional dimension, but still fundamental, expressed by the muses is the feminine point of view.

The website presents three issues, three poets and nine poems in total, organized in the following way: each issue is represented by a famous poet and for each poet, three poems are analysed.
The poems are readable in two different languages, one of which is always English, being the language in which the website has been developed.

The final purpose is to highlight the similarities between poetry of different and distant times and women, because humans' most intimate feelings remain the same, surviving time and space.
The poems can be visualized with underlined specific features and the whole website can wear different typographical styles, reflecting the various tendencies within historical periods.

For further information about the website structure, the texts (selection of poems and metadata) and the typographical styles, see the Documentation page.


Disclaimer

The purpose of this website is to explore various types of typographic and layout style for text documents, as an end-of-course project for the Information Modeling and Web technologies course of the Master Degree in Digital Humanities and Digital Knowledge of the University of Bologna, under prof. Fabio Vitali.

The documents contained in this web site have been selected for their length and complexity from external sources. Their publication here is not intended to be an alternative or replace their original locations.
